Changing Tides

Welcome to the page about my upcoming book, "Changing Tides: The Ways the Waves Take Us." Here, you'll find updates, exclusive previews, and more information about the book's release.

Summary:

Changing Tides is a story of triumph, as a young 18-year-old, goes on an unimaginable journey to chase his dream of surfing in the Gold Coast. Surviving a stroke, overcoming obstacles, and the unpredictable and ever-changing waves that life brings him.

Changing Tides is also a reflection on life, a metaphor for change and an homage to the main character's love for surfing. Each chapter takes the reader on a roller coaster ride of emotion and shows us all the ways the waves of life can take us, while sharing key lessons learned throughout the journey. Using personal surfing analogies, and references to the ocean, the author illustrates key themes like change, overcoming challenges, growth, letting go, self-love, freedom, and more throughout the book.

My Story: From Stroke to Stoke ü§ô

February 11, 2016

Shockingly one day I wake up and have a stroke. Numbness. Can't hold a pen. Slurring my speech. Losing my balance. So I go to the hospital, and they tell me I had a brain hemorrhage in my left thalamus. Unfortunately had to drop out of uni. [A picture of me 6 days post-stroke]

June 10, 2021

Eventually I graduate from my Kinesiology degree, despite many times wanting to give up. Crazy enough, the last 1.5 years of my degree were online because of COVID, but hey a degree is a degree. [A picture of me 6 years later posing for my grad picture during the pandemic]

June 10, 2022

The redemption year. Life returns back to normal (sort of). I feel the most normal I'd felt since my stroke. Had the time of my life, and dare I say I absolutely smashed it. [a picture of me at my IRL master's graduation ceremony]

June 23, 2022

Redemption year 2.0. COVID took away my Australia exchange dreams, so I took life into my own hands and went on a working holiday visa to the world of kangaroos and koalas. That's right it was time for a life of "no worries mate", surfing, and delaying "figuring out my life" as much as possible. [a picture right before I left for Australia]

Projects: My Crazy Ideas Brought to Life

Hoops 4 Health

Co-Founded Annual Basketball Tournament with the AEPi Fraternity to raise money for the Heart & Stroke Foundation for stroke research, to promote healthy lifestyles and to raise awareness for stroke. The event raised over $34K, secured 175+ donations, collaborated with 35+ sponsors, and attracted 1000+ attendees over the years.
